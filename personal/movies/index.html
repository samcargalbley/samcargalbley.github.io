Movie Reviews

Lost in Translation

A film about distance and recognition — less about romance than about two people briefly understanding each other at the exact moment they needed it.

— Sam Cargal-Bley

Aftersun

Memory reconstructed through fragments. The emotional weight arrives slowly and only fully lands after the film ends.

— Sam Cargal-Bley

Her

Warm and unsettling in equal measure. A story about intimacy and loneliness that treats technology as a mirror, not a gimmick.

— Sam Cargal-Bley

In the Mood for Love

Controlled, aching, and precise — two people circling what they want to say. Every frame feels like restraint made visible.

— Sam Cargal-Bley
